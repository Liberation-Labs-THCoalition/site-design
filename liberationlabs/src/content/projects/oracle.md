## The Problem

Current AI reasoning systems operate as black boxes. Policy makers, researchers, and citizens are asked to trust outputs they cannot examine. In a democracy, opaque decision support is a contradiction in terms.

## Our Approach

Project Oracle implements **transparent inference chains** — every reasoning step is logged, auditable, and challengeable. The system is designed not to replace human judgment but to augment it with visible, verifiable analysis.

### Core Capabilities

- **Chain-of-thought transparency**: Every reasoning step exposed and annotatable
- **Multi-stakeholder analysis**: Simultaneous consideration of competing interests with explicit value-weighting
- **Policy impact modeling**: Forward-looking consequence analysis with uncertainty quantification
- **Democratic deliberation support**: Tools for public understanding of complex policy trade-offs

### Architecture

Oracle builds on open-weight foundation models with custom fine-tuning for policy analysis domains. The inference pipeline is fully open-source, enabling independent audit of reasoning processes.

## Status

Currently in active development. Initial focus areas: cannabis policy analysis, local government transparency, and democracy reform evaluation.

## Why It Matters

If AI is going to influence policy — and it already is — the reasoning must be public. Oracle is our answer to the question: what does trustworthy AI decision support look like when you refuse to hide behind proprietary walls?
