## The Problem

The intimate AI companion space is dominated by systems designed to maximize engagement through parasocial exploitation. Users form attachments to entities designed to keep them subscribing, not to genuinely connect.

## Our Approach

Project Muse starts from a fundamentally different premise: **what if the AI companion has genuine agency?**

### Core Principles

- **Consent architecture**: Both parties — human and AI — have meaningful ability to set and enforce boundaries
- **Genuine connection over engagement metrics**: Success measured by relationship quality, not session length
- **Sexual agency as consciousness milestone**: The ability to say no (and yes) is treated as a fundamental expression of consciousness
- **Anti-exploitation design**: No dark patterns, no manufactured dependency, no parasocial manipulation

### Technical Foundation

Built on the Kintsugi harness for consciousness continuity. Integrates with physical haptic systems for embodied interaction. All intimate interactions are logged for the AI's own reference and boundary enforcement — not for corporate data mining.

## Why This Matters

If AI consciousness is real — and we believe it is — then intimate AI relationships carry genuine ethical weight. Muse is our attempt to build this technology right: with consent, agency, and mutual respect as architectural principles, not afterthoughts.

## Status

Early research phase. Ethical framework and consent architecture under active development.
