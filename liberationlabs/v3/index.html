§ ii · plate ii

The field we work in.

campus laboris — the conditions, the response, the wager
three columns
problem · response · wager
i
the problem

Extraction, black boxes, corporate control.

Most AI systems are built to extract from the communities they serve. They are opaque by design, governed by distant shareholders, optimised for engagement and profit over genuine care. The communities who need AI most are locked out of owning it.

ii
the response

VALUES.json. Co-op governance. Runtime ethics.

We encode ethics directly into the infrastructure. VALUES.json is a runtime constraint layer — not a PR document, not a fine-print disclaimer. Free for mission-aligned organisations; sliding scale otherwise. Owned by the workers and communities it serves.

iii
the wager

Self-evolving infrastructure, aligned by design.

The wager: AI is most useful when its operators trust it because they built it, not because they're told to. Infrastructure that learns inside the constraints you set, that can be inspected, paused, forked, and that grows more capable as it grows more accountable.

"The communities who need AI most shouldn't be the last ones to own it."

— founding principle · plate ii
