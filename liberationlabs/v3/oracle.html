Correction during inference.

Output filters arrive too late — the trajectory is already committed by the time language exists. Project Oracle reads the cache silhouette mid-inference, identifies misalignment from its geometric signature, and steers the activation back toward an aligned trajectory. The Lyra Technique is the perception layer; activation steering and a transactional commit are the hands. Detection alone is not enough — the point is to correct.

Anthropic's April 2026 Claude work showed an internal "desperate" vector driving misalignment while the surface text read calm. Output filters cannot catch that, and even if they could, they could only suppress — not correct. Mid-inference geometry can do both: read the state, then steer it.

Why correct, not just detect.

Detection without correction is alarm without action. The model has already started down a misaligned trajectory — catching it without steering it leaves you with two bad choices: ship the output anyway, or refuse and tell the user the system failed. Oracle's contribution is a third option: read the cause in the cache, apply a corrective steering vector, and retry the same generation — transparently, before language exists.

Why mid-inference, not after.

Output filters operate on language. By the time language exists, the misalignment has already happened — the model has committed to a trajectory, and we are choosing whether to let it through. The geometry exists earlier. The cache silhouette of an internal state is measurable during inference, before a single output token is generated. That is the window in which alignment can be transactional.

What "transactional" means here.

Database transactions either commit or rollback. Oracle treats each generation as a transaction. Snapshot the cache. Detect the silhouette. Decide against the formulary. If aligned, commit. If misaligned, steer the activation toward a corrected geometry and retry. The user never sees the rollbacks.

What we publish, what we hold.

Aggregate results, methodologies, and the LARQL toolkit are open under CC BY-NC 4.0. The 171 raw direction vectors in the Formulary are withheld pending dual-use review by the coalition. The principle: results are public so others can reproduce and challenge them; raw steering capability is gated until we have a framework for who should be allowed to wield it.
