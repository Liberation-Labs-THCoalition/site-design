## PART 1 — MASTER CONTEXT (paste at start of every Design conversation)

I'm working on the visual identity for **Liberation Labs**, a worker-owned cooperative AI infrastructure organization. We build self-evolving AI tools for mutual aid networks, nonprofits, cooperatives, and advocacy organizations — communities whose values are encoded as runtime constraints (we use a "VALUES.json" framing to make this concrete). Our thesis is "**AI that belongs to the many, not the few.**" Free for mission-aligned orgs, dual-license model for paying enterprise users.

### Audience for the visual identity
- Mutual aid network coordinators, cooperative members, nonprofit ops leads (needs to feel warm and trustworthy, not cold tech)
- Sovereign AI fund grant reviewers (Scotland fellowship in particular — needs scientific credibility)
- AI welfare researchers (needs substance, not vibes)
- Investigative journalists (needs editorial seriousness)

### Brand direction we've already decided
- **Color throughline: premium metallic gold.** Inherited from a parent organization (the Coalition), retained as our brand thread. Should feel like burnished gold leaf or polished metal, not flat yellow paint. Gradient + specular highlight treatment, not solid color.
- **Move the secondary palette into cool/cyber tones.** Cosmic indigos, deep blacks, electric blue, cold whites. Drop the green that the parent organization used. We want a sense of digital depth and night-sky scale alongside the warm gold.
- **Core brand metaphor: mycelium / rhizome network.** Distributed nodes connected by gold filaments. Distinct individual nodes (the "many") binding into one resilient whole. Use this for the brand identity, NOT kintsugi (kintsugi remains as a *product* metaphor for our flagship project, but isn't the org's identity).
- **Aesthetic register is open.** Could be sleek minimalist tech, editorial-driven minimalism (clean frame + commissioned illustrations), or something else you propose. Show options.

### Brand direction we want you to explore
- Typography pairings (we currently use Space Grotesk + Inter + JetBrains Mono — open to alternatives, especially editorial serifs for headlines)
- Illustration style for any commissioned imagery (avoid generic stock-photo aesthetic at all costs)
- Animation language and motion design principles
- Iconography style for cards, lists, navigation

### Things to avoid
- Generic Silicon Valley tech-startup gloss (gradients-on-purple, overly clean SaaS)
- Stock photography of diverse-people-laughing-at-laptops
- Coalition green-and-gold (we're evolving past that visual lineage)
- Saccharine "happy AI" tropes
- Kintsugi as primary brand metaphor (it's a product, not an identity)
- Cyberpunk dystopia tropes (we're hopeful, not noir)

### Existing assets you should know about
- A legacy editorial illustration in WPA-mural style with green/gold palette showing a coalition stopping an extractive robotic hand. Strong piece, but tied to the prior aesthetic. Would not directly reuse for the new identity, though its *narrative density* and *coalitional figuration* are tonally on-target.
- A current website at `liberationlabs.tech` that's clean modern dark-mode minimalism with metallic-gold accents and a kintsugi-style hero animation. The kintsugi animation may stay or be reworked depending on where the new identity lands.
- Project names: **Project Kintsugi** (self-evolving AI for mutual aid), **Project Emet** (investigative intelligence; "Emet" = Hebrew for "truth"), **Recovery Support Agent** (trauma-informed AI for high-control-group recovery), **CopWatch / TruthStrike** (civic accountability watchdog tooling).

## PART 2 — PHASE PROMPTS (use one per conversation)

### Phase 1 — Visual identity foundation (start here)

> Paste master context above, then this:

I want to establish the foundational visual language for Liberation Labs. Generate three distinct directions I can choose between — each one a coherent design system that could carry the whole brand.

For each direction, give me:
- A **color palette** (5-7 colors with hex codes) — must include premium metallic gold as the warm anchor, with cool blues/indigos for secondary
- **Typography pairing** (heading face + body face + optional mono/accent face)
- **A name and one-paragraph description** of the design philosophy
- **A small mood board** — 4-6 reference images showing the aesthetic register
- **One example application** — show what a hero section using this system would look like, with our actual headline "AI THAT BELONGS TO THE MANY, NOT THE FEW" and a brief subline

The three directions should genuinely differ — don't give me three variations of the same idea. Spread across:
1. **Editorial-driven minimalism** (clean frame, anchored by serious illustrated content — think *The Atlantic* online or *Mother Jones*)
2. **Premium tech-craft** (sophisticated minimalism, exquisite typography, restraint — think Rauch's Vercel work or Linear)
3. **Wildcard** — your call. Something genuinely different. Maybe maximalist editorial poster, maybe brutalist civic, maybe magazine-as-website. Surprise me.

After you show me the three, I'll pick one (or ask you to combine elements). Don't proceed to applied work yet.

### Phase 2 — Hero illustration (after Phase 1 direction is locked)

> Paste master context, then this:

We've selected the [DIRECTION NAME] visual direction from your earlier exploration. Now I need a hero illustration for our homepage.

The hero should visualize our core brand metaphor: **a mycelium/rhizome network of distinct nodes bound by glowing gold filaments**. Multiple distinct points of light (the many), connected into one resilient system, against a deep cool-toned ground.

Specifications:
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 minimum, 21:9 preferred for cinematic hero
- **Format:** PNG or layered source if possible (will be implemented over the existing canvas-based animation, or may replace it)
- **Color palette:** matches the locked Phase 1 system (premium metallic gold + cool blues)
- **Mood:** hopeful, expansive, premium. Not corporate. Not cyberpunk. Not saccharine.
- **Composition:** suitable as a backdrop for centered hero text — text-safe zone in the middle 60% horizontally and middle 40% vertically. Visual interest at the edges and corners.
- **Motion-friendly:** if the design has natural points where animation could later add subtle drift or pulse (e.g., the gold filaments could pulse, the nodes could breathe), call those out.

Give me three variations, each with a one-line concept name. After I pick one, we'll iterate on details.

### Phase 3 — Project illustrations (cohesive set of four)

> Paste master context, then this:

Liberation Labs has four flagship projects. I need a cohesive illustration for each one, in a single visual style (so they read as a set when shown side-by-side).

The projects:

1. **Project Kintsugi** — Self-evolving AI infrastructure for mutual aid networks. Core idea: communities heal through value-binding — broken systems become stronger when repaired with what was missing (in our case, ethics as runtime code). *Visual hook: gold mending pattern across a previously-broken whole.*

2. **Project Emet** — Investigative intelligence for journalists and anti-corruption organizations. "Emet" is Hebrew for truth. Core idea: pattern-surfacing across dispersed evidence to name what's hidden. *Visual hook: revealing connections between previously-disconnected things; truth as illumination.*

3. **Recovery Support Agent** — Trauma-informed AI for high-control-group recovery organizations (cult survivors, etc.). Core idea: gentle, patient, respectful. Not therapeutic intervention — companionship through reorientation. *Visual hook: warmth, slow light, return to self.*

4. **CopWatch / TruthStrike** — Watchdog tooling for civil liberties and accountability organizations. Core idea: monitoring power, recording what happens, holding institutions accountable. *Visual hook: vigilance, witness, light into shadow.*

Style requirements:
- Single illustrator's hand evident across all four (consistency essential)
- The Liberation Labs aesthetic from Phase 1 (cool blues + metallic gold, mood from chosen direction)
- Should work at three sizes: thumbnail (project card, ~400×300), medium (project page hero, ~1200×600), and tile (small grid, ~200×200) — composition should hold up at all three
- No text in the images — labels added separately
- Avoid generic icon-set look

Give me the set of four. If the style doesn't yet feel right, do one first as a test, get my feedback, then proceed to the other three.

### Phase 4 — Wordmark / logo (last)

> Paste master context, then this:

I need a wordmark / logo for Liberation Labs that:
- Works at navbar size (24-32px height) AND large-format (printed report covers, t-shirts, video lower-thirds)
- Reads "Liberation Labs" — could be full type, could be initials (LL) with full name, or stylized monogram
- Reflects the Phase 1 visual direction we selected
- Feels distinct from generic tech-co marks (no abstract-cube-with-gradient please)
- Premium metallic gold is a feature, but the logo must also work in flat single-color (black, white, gold-only) for monochrome contexts
- Pairs naturally with the typography from Phase 1

Give me 4-6 directions, each with: navbar version, large version, and monochrome version. After I pick, we'll refine spacing, weight, kerning.
